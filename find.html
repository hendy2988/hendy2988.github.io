Where to Start

When beginning the search for a new occupation, many individuals don't even know where to start looking! Below we have listed several different sources an individual can check when looking for available local employment options!

Before even beginning to search for job openings, it's a good idea to evaluate your own skills and interests. If you know you'll enjoy the job you're applying for, you are more likely to seem enthusiastic in your resumes and interviews, which will in turn help you seem more fit for the job for which you're applying, making you a more eligible candidate!

Being familiar with your skill set can also help narrow down job options before you begin your search. For example, if you have a degree in computer science and are a skilled typist with profiency in navigating the internet, you probably shouldn't go apply for a job working construction. Becoming familiar with your capabilities can save both you and any potential employers loads of time when you finally begin the search for your job!


Check the Web!

There are many online sources that allow various companies to list their open employment options. Checking these internet sites can be a good start when searching for a new job near you! Most of the sites are even readily accessible and mobile-friendly! Below, we have constructed a short list of good sites that would serve as ideal places to begin your search:

  • LinkedIn
    A “professional recruitment website”. This website is incredibly useful for job category searches and open employment options, but can also be used as a way to get in touch with former colleagues and employers, making it useful for references and letters of recommendation.

  • Indeed
    This app offers a wide variety of job options and listings and allows you to use a search option to narrow down which jobs will be best for you! This site even assists you through the entire process of getting a job, from searching for a position near you to applying for it.

  • Monster
    This site is essentially a thorough documentation of all available job listings world-wide! This app acts a miniature search engine to allow the user to find jobs near him/her!

  • Careerbuilder
    Careerbuilder is a site that best matches your interests and talents with the right employers who would best be in need of your skillset! It’s very individual and personalized and essentially acts as a sort of career-placement test!

  • Snagajob
    Snagajob utilizes over 300,000 employer locations to help potential employers connect with searching employees within seconds!

  • HigherEdJobs
    This site is primarily and information-based source for users to learn more about different salaries, requirements, and availabilities of different careers.

Of course, generalized sites aren't the only web resources you can use to further your search for a job.
